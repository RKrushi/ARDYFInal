Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions Answered

Can foreigners buy property in Dubai?+

Yes — UAE law allows 100% foreign freehold ownership of properties in designated areas across Dubai. International investors enjoy the same legal protections as UAE nationals, with titles registered with the Dubai Land Department.

What rental yields can I expect in Dubai?+

Dubai typically delivers rental yields of 7–12% per annum, significantly higher than major markets like London, New York, or Singapore. Studio and 1-bedroom units in prime areas often achieve the highest yields, frequently exceeding 10%.

What is the minimum investment amount?+

Entry-level studios in emerging communities such as JVC and Arjan start from around AED 400,000–600,000. Premium areas like Downtown Dubai or Business Bay typically start from AED 900,000+. Off-plan properties often require just 10–20% deposit during construction.

Is there a property tax in Dubai?+

No — Dubai charges zero income tax, zero capital gains tax, and zero inheritance tax on property. The only government charges are a one-time 4% Dubai Land Department transfer fee and an annual service charge for maintenance of communal areas.

What is the UAE Golden Visa?+

The UAE Golden Visa is a long-term residency visa (5 or 10 years) available to property investors who purchase a property worth AED 2 million or more. It grants the right to live, work, and study in the UAE for the investor and immediate family members.

How does ARDY help international investors?+

ARDY provides end-to-end support — from property selection and developer liaison through to legal documentation, DLD registration, mortgage advice, and post-purchase property management. We offer virtual consultations and tours for overseas clients.
