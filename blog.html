Common Questions Answered

Can foreigners buy property in Dubai?+

Yes — UAE law allows 100% foreign freehold ownership in designated areas. International investors enjoy the same legal protections as UAE nationals, with titles registered with the Dubai Land Department.

What rental yields can I expect in Dubai?+

Dubai typically delivers rental yields of 7–12% per annum, significantly higher than London, New York, or Singapore. Studio and 1-bedroom units in prime areas often achieve 10%+.

What is the minimum investment amount?+

Entry-level studios in emerging communities start from AED 400,000–600,000. Premium areas like Downtown or Business Bay start from AED 900,000+. Off-plan properties often require just 10–20% deposit.

Is there a property tax in Dubai?+

No — Dubai charges zero income tax, zero capital gains tax, and zero inheritance tax on property. Only a one-time 4% DLD transfer fee and an annual service charge apply.

What is the UAE Golden Visa?+

A long-term 5 or 10-year residency visa available to property investors purchasing AED 2 million+ property. Grants the right to live, work, and study in the UAE for the investor and family.
